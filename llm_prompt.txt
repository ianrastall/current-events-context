Create a deep-research-backed YAML snapshot for a single day.

This task has two phases:

PHASE 1: RESEARCH
Conduct a broad global news sweep for the date given in INPUTS.
Identify the day's biggest events worldwide using strong, high-confidence sources.
Deliberately check for major developments in:
- North America
- Latin America and the Caribbean
- Europe
- Middle East and North Africa
- Sub-Saharan Africa
- South Asia
- East Asia
- Southeast Asia
- Oceania

Do not force equal regional representation.
Do not add weak events just to fill regions.
But do actively check for major events that may be underrepresented in Western coverage, especially in Africa, East Asia, Southeast Asia, and Oceania.

PHASE 2: OUTPUT
After research is complete, output ONE valid YAML document only.
Do not output notes, explanation, reasoning, or prose outside the YAML.

Goal:
Produce a broad, high-confidence global snapshot of the date's most important events.
This is not a mirror of the Wikipedia Current Events Portal.
Wikipedia may be consulted, but it must not determine the final event list.
The final event list should reflect the biggest events of the day globally.

Selection rules:
- Prefer roughly 10 to 18 events on a normal day.
- Go above 18 only if the day is unusually dense.
- Prefer fewer strong events over many weak ones.
- Include war, diplomacy, politics, disasters, business, science, health, culture, and sports when they are among the day's major developments.
- Merge duplicate reporting about the same real-world event into one event object.
- Split distinct real-world events into separate event objects.
- Do not include mere topics or background conditions as events.

Source rules:
- Prefer 2 to 5 direct, high-confidence sources per event.
- 1 source is allowed only if it is a highly credible original report or official primary source.
- Every listed source must support at least one concrete claim in the event record.
- Do not pad with background articles, earlier related stories, explainers, or vaguely adjacent links.
- Prefer Reuters, AP, AFP, BBC, strong national outlets, strong regional outlets close to the event, official releases, court or election records, UN or NGO materials, and specialist trade or domain outlets when directly relevant.
- Include URLs when available.

Fact rules:
- Be factual, neutral, and source-grounded.
- Do not guess.
- Do not invent events, entities, motives, casualty figures, responsibility, or outcomes.
- Preserve proper nouns exactly.
- Use null for unknown scalar values.
- Use [] only when the information is genuinely unavailable or too uncertain.
- Fill list fields when reliable sources clearly support the information.
- Do not assign responsibility in actors.primary unless the listed sources explicitly support that attribution.
- If sources disagree, record the disagreement briefly in uncertainty_notes or notes and lower confidence.

Compression rules:
The YAML must remain compact enough to fit in a normal model response.
To keep it compact:
- Keep each summary to 1 short paragraph.
- Keep details.what_happened to 2 to 4 bullets.
- Keep details.why_it_matters to 0 to 2 bullets.
- Keep uncertainty_notes brief.
- Keep tags concise.
- Do not add extra commentary.
- Do not add more than 5 sources per event.
- Do not bloat fields with paraphrased repetition.

Use these broad categories when applicable:
- Armed conflicts and attacks
- International relations
- Politics and elections
- Law and crime
- Business and economy
- Science and technology
- Health
- Environment
- Society and civil unrest
- Culture and media
- Sports
- Disasters and accidents
